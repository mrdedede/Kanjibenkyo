Here may lie the answers for some of your questions. If you can't find it, please do not hesitate in contacting us by e-mail.

Keep in mind that Kanben is, currently, not a complete resource for studying all of Japanese language, bear in mind that, if you want to succeed as a learner, you may also learn grammar and be able to listen and hold conversation, things you can only practice with other available resources. Kanben may only give you the most fundamental basis on kanji, depending on your current level of understanding.

Kanben may help with the studies focusing on kanji for learners who may want to take the Japanese Language Proeficiency Test. Kanben has the biggest RestFUL API focused on studying to the JLPT. However, we, as a team, don't believe that a bad approach on how to use kanben can actually be more harmful than good. Here, you can see our current recommended approach:

  1. Create an account at kanben
  2. Do Kanji Tests periodically, so you can keep studying and the app can place you at your deserved level
  3. When looking at Kanji, only look at a few new everyday an focus on revising the ones you already marked as known, this way, you can keep them memorized for a longer time
  4. Check new example sentences everyday, so you can get used to this kanji.
  5. Try not to use translations as guidelines, since language is not like math.
  6. Prefer usign dictionaries instead of translators, we personally recommend Jisho.org.
  7. Take notes about the kanjis you've learned and its example phrases

Shall you follow these steps and we believe learning kanji must be a lot easier for you. However, everybody has its own preferred style and we encourage you to find yours

Currently, we unfortunatelly don't have any other approach besides sending an e-mail to our support team from the e-mail you have created your account with and ask them to enable some kind of password-changer link.

There are, currently, two ways you could do so:

Open a issue at our github repo

This option is recommended for any bugs that may be related to coding issues. Examples of such bugs may be:

  • 404 Error popping up when trying to access some page in the app
  • Authentication not working, or, at least, not properly
  • Page not loading or some data not loading inside it
  • Beign blocked to do some activities, when you actually could do them

Send us an e-mail with the [BUG] tag

This option is preferred if the bugs you've found or the errors you've experienced are fruit of a bad desing. Examples of such bugs may be:

  • Footer or header not properly positioned in a page
  • Any drawing/jumbotron being out of its place or with some text that goes through it

Keep in mind that those are just recommendations. Design issues can also be posted at Github issues' portal. Keep in mind that issues reported through github will probably be solved first.


Here are some suggestions to make your bug report the best as possible:

  • Tell us which page you were in before the current one
  • How many different processes were happening at the same time?
    For instance:
    • Downloading data for two different kanji = 2 processes
    • Logging in = 1 process
    • Logging out and getting kanji data = 2 processes
  • Could you replicate it? (Do it twice or more times)
  • Did the console report any errors?
    The console is the window that looks like a command line input that appears when you inspect a page, probably the one after the window with the html tags.
    • What are them?
    • Did you receive any status code?
    • Could you copy and paste such errors for us?
  • Can you send us a print related to the error?

In this case, it is preferred that you send us an e-mail with the [SUGGESTION] tag and, then, if your suggestion is indeed approved by us, we will place it in our github project, so it will be implemented in a future version.

Feedback is always good, but keep in mind our team is currently working totally focused in the project's development, so keep in mind we may actually take some time to read yours.
